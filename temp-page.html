Temperature vs Latitude




An often noted weather assumption is that latitudes closest to the equator have higher maximum temperatures compared to countries with higher latitudes. The WeatherPy data set can specifically address this assumption by correlating the latitudes of 557 random cities with maximum temperatures for a specific date. The use of 11/11/2019, for example, represents winter in the northern hemisphere and summer in the Southern Hemisphere. We would expect the countries with southern latitudes to have higher temperatures than the countries with northern latitudes, and the latitudes closest to the equator to have the highest maximum temperatures. These predictions were corroborated by the WeatherPy data, which shows significantly higher maximum temperatures as latitude approaches the equator (Latitude=0). Maximum temperatures in the Southern Hemisphere (summer) were also observed to be elevated compared to maximum temperatures in the Northern Hemisphere (winter). Future correlative experiments should consider dates where summer and winter are inverted compared to these data.
